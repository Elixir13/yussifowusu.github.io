ABOUT ME

I am a Medical Laboratory Scientist, a Biomedical Researcher and a Data Analyst with a growing interest in epidemiology and how diseases affect populations. Most of my work has focused on infectious diseases like schistosomiasis and malaria, where I combine laboratory work with data analysis to understand patterns of transmission and risk.

I’m especially interested in how environmental and climate factors influence disease spread, and how data can be used to improve diagnosis, surveillance, and public health decisions. Part of my work also involves conducting and contributing to systematic and scoping reviews, helping to synthesize existing evidence and identify research gaps.

I work with tools like Python, SPSS, Excel, Power BI, and QGIS to explore data, visualize trends, and communicate findings clearly. I enjoy turning complex health data into practical insights and I’m always open to learning, collaboration, and meaningful research opportunities.

PUBLICATIONS

Published articles and Articles under review

Impact of Climate Change on Schistosomiasis Transmission and Distribution—Scoping Review

Kwame Kumi Asare, Muhi-Deen Wonwana Mohammed, Yussif Owusu Aboagye, Kathrin Arndts, and Manuel Ritter International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health., 2025; 22(5):812. DOI: doi.org/10.3390/ijerph22050812

This study synthesizes current knowledge on the influence of climate variables (temperature, rainfall, water bodies) on snail populations, transmission dynamics, and the shifting geographic range of schistosomiasis. It also explores the potential effects of climate adaptation policies on disease control.

Mapping the Threat: A Scoping Review of Acinetobacter baumannii-Associated Hospital Infections and Resistance Mechanisms

Yussif Owusu Aboagye, Muhi-Deen Wonwana Mohammed, and Kwame Kumi Asare
International Journal of Microbiology UNDER REVIEW

This scoping review synthesizes global evidence on the epidemiological distribution of A. baumannii-associated HAIs, the genetic mechanisms underlying its multidrug-resistant (MDR) and extensively drug-resistant (XDR) phenotypes, and the structural interconnectivity of resistance determinants.

Evidence Gaps and Global Patterns in Leishmaniasis Control: A Scoping Review of Clinical, Diagnostic and Treatment Strategies

Muhi-Deen Wonwana Mohammed, Yussif Owusu Aboagye,, Akwasi Anyanful, Samuel Kyei, and Kwame Kumi Asare
Journal of Parasitology Research UNDER REVIEW

This scoping review maps the global distribution and proportional burden of different clinical forms of leishmaniasis, evaluates the diagnostic tools used and their performance, and assesses treatment modalities, outcomes, and relapse patterns.

False-Positive and False-Negative Malaria Rapid Diagnostic Test Results: Evidence from a Nationally Representative Survey of Children in Ghana

Kwame Kumi Asare, Yussif Owusu Aboagye, Muhi-Deen Wonwana Mohammed, Margaret Darko, Christian Adjei, Honey Fumilayo Emmanuels, Annobil Nana Boakye, Ebenezer Asiedu
Malaria Journal UNDER REVIEW

This study estimated malaria prevalence using microscopy and RDTs, evaluated the diagnostic performance of RDTs relative to microscopy, and identified socio-demographic and household-level predictors of false-positive and false-negative RDT results among Ghanaian children under five years.
