Geospatial Modeling of Schistosomiasis Transmission Risks

Predictive modeling integrating climate variability, geospatial data, and machine learning to identify high-risk zones in Sub-Saharan Africa under future climate scenarios.

  • Python
  • GeoPandas
  • Scikit-learn
  • ArcGIS
  • R

Project Overview

This project developed a robust predictive model to forecast schistosomiasis transmission risks, focusing on the impacts of climate change. By combining environmental data (temperature, precipitation, and hydrology) with machine learning algorithms, the model achieved approximately 85% accuracy in identifying high-risk areas.

Methodology

Geospatial data processing was performed using GeoPandas and ArcGIS. Machine learning models (random forests and gradient boosting) were trained with Scikit-learn to incorporate climate projections from multiple scenarios.

Results & Visualizations

The model generated interactive risk maps and dashboards, highlighting zones requiring targeted public health interventions and resource allocation.

Outcomes & Impact

Key findings were presented at the 2024 ASTMH Annual Meeting in collaboration with the WHO Regional Office. The work contributed to evidence-based strategies for schistosomiasis control in climate-vulnerable regions.
